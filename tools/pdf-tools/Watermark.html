Add a Text or Image Watermark to Any PDF

A watermark is a semi-transparent label placed over every page of a document. It signals ownership, marks a file as a draft or confidential copy, or carries a recipient's name to discourage forwarding. This tool stamps a custom watermark across every page of your PDF — using text, your logo, or both — with full control over position, opacity, rotation, and size. Everything runs in your browser.

When You Should Watermark a PDF

  • Drafts and review copies: A diagonal "DRAFT" or "FOR REVIEW ONLY" watermark stops reviewers from forwarding the file as final.
  • Confidential documents: Mark proposals, contracts, and internal reports with "CONFIDENTIAL" or the recipient's name to trace leaks.
  • Brand protection: Photographers, designers, and consultants overlay a logo on shared portfolios so previews remain identifiable if they spread online.
  • Legal hold: Lawyers and HR teams add "ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED" or "DO NOT DISTRIBUTE" stamps required by company policy.
  • Versioning: A timestamp or version number watermark helps you tell apart copies of the same document at a glance.

Text vs. Image Watermarks

Text watermarks are lightweight, scale cleanly, and are the right choice for short labels like "DRAFT" or "PAID". Pick a bold font, a low opacity (15–30%), and a diagonal rotation of around 45 degrees for the classic stamp look. Image watermarks suit logos, company seals, or signatures. Use a PNG with transparency so the background of each page shows through naturally. Keep the image well below the page width — usually 30–40% — so it acts as an overlay, not a cover.

Positioning, Opacity, and Repeat

Position controls where the watermark sits: centred is the most common choice, but corners work well for logos and a tiled pattern repeats the watermark across the entire page for maximum coverage. Opacity between 15% and 40% keeps the document readable while still making the watermark obvious. Rotating text 30 to 45 degrees draws the eye and is harder to crop out than a horizontal label. The live preview lets you adjust all of these before generating the final file.

Important: Watermarks Are Not Encryption

A watermark is a visual deterrent, not security. A determined user can still copy the text, screenshot pages, or use editing tools to remove the watermark layer — especially when the watermark sits on top of empty whitespace. If you need real protection against copying, combine watermarking with password protection in a desktop PDF editor, or convert the watermarked PDF to a flattened image-only PDF using our PDF to Image tool and rebuild it as a PDF.

Privacy

Documents you watermark may contain sensitive content — contracts, IP, or personal records. This tool runs everything locally using pdf-lib. No file or logo is uploaded to a server. You can confirm this by watching the Network tab in DevTools while you generate the watermarked file.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will the watermark appear on every page?
Yes. The same watermark is applied to every page at the position, size, and opacity you choose. You can re-run the tool on selected pages by first splitting the document with our Split PDF tool.

Can I add a watermark behind the existing content instead of on top?
Yes. There is a toggle to place the watermark in the background layer. Bear in mind that for scanned PDFs the page content is itself an image covering the entire page, so a background watermark will be hidden.

What format should my logo be in?
PNG with transparency is best. JPG works but the rectangular background will be visible. SVG is not currently supported in the watermark layer; convert it to a high-resolution PNG first.

Will the original PDF be modified?
No. The tool produces a new PDF for download. Your source file stays untouched on your device.

Does the watermark stay readable when printed?
Yes, provided you do not push opacity below 15%. Very light watermarks can disappear on standard printers. If you plan to print, preview at 100% zoom first.
