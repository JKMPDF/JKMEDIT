Combine Multiple PDF Files into One Document

Merging PDF files is one of the most common document tasks for office workers, students, and freelancers. Whether you are bundling invoices for a client, joining scanned chapters of a textbook, or combining contract pages signed across different days, you need a way to stitch them together cleanly without losing formatting. This tool lets you upload several PDFs, reorder pages by drag and drop, deselect any page you do not want, and download a single merged file — all directly inside your browser.

How the Merge Works Under the Hood

The merger is built on top of pdf-lib and pdf.js, two well-known open-source libraries maintained by Mozilla and the PDF community. When you select files, each one is parsed in your browser into its individual pages. You can then rearrange those pages visually before clicking Merge PDF. The final document is generated locally and offered as a download. Because the entire process runs on your device, your files never reach our server, which matters when documents contain sensitive details like ID copies, payslips, or contracts.

Common Use Cases

  • Tax and accounting: Combine receipts, bank statements, and Form 16 into a single submission package.
  • Job applications: Merge resume, cover letter, certificates, and portfolio into one PDF that recruiters can open in a single click.
  • Legal paperwork: Join signature pages, agreements, and addendums into the order required by the contract.
  • Academic submissions: Bundle assignment cover sheet, references, and attached research papers for a clean submission.
  • Invoices and reports: Compile a month of invoices or a multi-section report for archival or printing.

Tips for a Cleaner Merge

A few things make a noticeable difference in the final output. First, name your input files in the order you want them merged before uploading — it makes reordering inside the tool much faster, especially when you have ten or more documents. Second, if any source PDF is password-protected, remove the password before uploading; encrypted PDFs cannot be parsed by browser libraries for security reasons. Third, if your final file feels heavier than expected, run it through our Compress PDF tool afterwards to reduce size without changing layout.

How This Tool Compares to Desktop Software

Adobe Acrobat, Foxit, and similar paid applications offer merging as part of much larger suites. They are excellent for editing text inside PDFs, redacting content, or running OCR — tasks this tool does not aim to replace. For pure merging and reordering, however, a browser tool is faster to open, requires no installation, and works on Chromebooks, iPads, and shared office computers where you cannot install software. If you only need merging a few times a month, paying a yearly subscription is hard to justify.

Privacy and File Handling

Files stay on your device. The page does not upload your PDFs to any backend, and we do not log filenames or content. You can verify this yourself by opening browser DevTools, switching to the Network tab, and watching the merge — you will not see any outgoing request carrying your file. Once you close or refresh the tab, the in-memory PDF data is cleared by the browser.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a limit on the number of PDFs I can merge?
There is no fixed limit imposed by the tool. The practical limit depends on your device's available memory. On a typical laptop you can comfortably merge dozens of files totalling a few hundred megabytes.

Will the merged PDF keep the original formatting, fonts, and images?
Yes. Pages are copied at the binary level, so fonts, images, vector graphics, and form fields remain intact. The merged file is visually identical to the originals, just combined.

Can I merge password-protected PDFs?
Not directly. Open the file in a PDF viewer, remove the password by saving a copy, and then merge that copy. This is a deliberate safety constraint shared by every browser-based PDF library.

Does the order of files I select determine the final order?
It is the starting order, but you can rearrange pages individually by dragging them inside the preview grid before clicking merge.

Is the tool free for commercial use?
Yes. There is no usage limit and no watermark added to your output. The page is supported by display advertising, which is what keeps the tool free.
