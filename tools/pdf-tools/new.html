A Private, Free Way to Extract PDF Text into Word

If you've ever received a locked PDF or a scanned document and needed to edit the text inside, you know the struggle. Typing out pages of data manually is a waste of your valuable time. We built this entirely client-side PDF to Word OCR utility to solve that problem without asking you to pay monthly subscriptions or upload your private documents to third-party servers.

How It Works 100% In Your Browser

Unlike other converters that take your file, upload it to a remote server, process it, and send it back, our tool leverages the processing power of your own device. When you upload your PDF, our tool uses Optical Character Recognition (OCR) powered by Tesseract.js directly inside your browser. It scans each page as an image, extracts the text, and compiles it into a cleanly structured .docx file using Docx.js. The process ensures absolute privacy because your file never actually leaves your computer.

Real-World Scenarios: Who is this for?

  • Students & Researchers: Extracting text from scanned library books and research papers without manually retyping quotes.
  • Privacy-Conscious Professionals: Extracting text from sensitive financial reports, legal contracts, or medical records where uploading to external servers is a security risk.
  • Administrative Staff: Quickly pulling data from old records into editable formats.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the visual layout kept exactly the same?
Because this is a free, privacy-focused text extraction tool, it focuses on accurately recovering your text. Complex graphics, side-by-side columns, and specific fonts are stripped away in favor of giving you a clean, editable text document in Word. If you need perfect visual cloning, professional desktop software is recommended, but for pulling data, this is extremely effective.

Why does it take a little longer for large files?
The OCR engine reads your document like a human does—by scanning the shapes of the letters. Since it uses your device's processor to do this securely, a 50-page scanned document will take a bit longer to process than a standard 2-page text PDF.
