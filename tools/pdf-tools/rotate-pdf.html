Rotate PDF Pages — Fix Sideways or Upside-Down Documents

Scanned PDFs often come out sideways or upside-down because the original page was placed in the wrong orientation in the scanner or photographed from an awkward angle on a phone. Most PDF viewers let you rotate pages on screen but do not actually save the rotation. This tool permanently rotates pages — by 90°, 180°, or 270° — and writes a new PDF where every page is correctly oriented for reading, printing, and sharing.

When Rotation Is the Right Fix

  • Phone-scanned documents: Pages photographed in landscape often need a 90° rotation to read properly.
  • Mixed-orientation scans: A multi-page scan where pages 3 and 7 came out upside-down because the feeder grabbed them backwards.
  • Spreadsheet exports: Wide spreadsheets exported to PDF as landscape but expected to be viewed portrait by the recipient.
  • Architectural drawings: Plans saved at the wrong orientation that need to match the title block layout.
  • Pre-print preparation: Ensuring all pages are oriented correctly before sending to a print shop, which often charges extra for orientation fixes.

Rotate All Pages or Selected Pages

You can apply a uniform rotation to the entire document — useful when an entire scan came out sideways — or rotate only specific pages. The page preview lets you click each thumbnail and pick its rotation independently. This is the right mode when a document mixes correctly-oriented pages with a few stragglers that need fixing.

How Rotation Works Inside a PDF

PDF rotation is stored as metadata on each page rather than by actually re-drawing the content. The tool uses pdf-lib to update each page's rotation flag and writes a new file. Because content is not re-rasterised, file size barely changes and text stays fully searchable and selectable. This is different from rotating an image, where the pixels themselves are remapped.

Tips for a Clean Result

Decide rotation by looking at how text reads, not at thumbnails — thumbnails can be misleading. If your scanner produces consistently rotated output, fix the scanner orientation setting once instead of correcting every batch. For documents created from word processors that came out sideways, it is usually better to re-export from the source than to rotate afterwards, since rotating does not change the page dimensions and a portrait page rotated to landscape will leave content compressed inside an awkward aspect ratio.

Privacy

Rotation happens locally inside your browser. No upload step is required, so the contents of your document stay on your device. You can confirm this by opening DevTools and watching the Network tab while you rotate and download.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I rotate a single page in a long PDF?
Yes. The preview lets you select individual pages and set rotation per page. Pages not selected keep their original orientation.

Will my text still be searchable after rotation?
Yes. Rotation updates page metadata, not page content. Selectable text, hyperlinks, and form fields all carry over.

What is the difference between 90° clockwise and 270°?
They produce the same visual result. Some tools label them differently. 90° clockwise = 270° counter-clockwise; either one rotates a portrait page so the top edge is on the right.

The rotation looks fine on screen but is wrong when I print. Why?
Your printer driver may be auto-rotating based on page dimensions. Open the print dialog and look for an "Orientation" or "Auto-rotate" option and disable it.

Can rotation fix a tilted scan (a few degrees off)?
No. This tool only does 90° increments. For tilted scans, you need a deskew feature, which is available in OCR software like Adobe Acrobat Pro or open-source tools like ScanTailor.
