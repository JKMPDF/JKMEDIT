A Solution to Accidental Deletion of Documents when Editing

A well-known problem when editing digital files is the fact that an unwanted deletion is so easy to commit. With a regular word processor, the possibility of accidentally deleting a sentence with the intention of adding a comment is too high. The PDF Note Pad functions on a layer above the document. The original PDF is treated as background image information that cannot be corrupted by the annotation process.

This "layering" of information is absolutely essential when working with legal documents, historical records, or the final version of a report. You can write all over the page but you cannot corrupt the original text of the..."DOCUMENT" (unless you edit it with a different tool). It gives reviewers peace of mind when they feel like being critical or messy. The author and the reviewer can be two different people without risking the corruption of the source file. I'm sure the "author" is going to appreciate that after the reviewer finishes over-writing the document, his notes can be stamped out or flattened. The document will be squeaky clean.
